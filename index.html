My Photography Adventure

I did not push the shutter with color in mind

Introduction

Tri-X 400. That was the film I used before I started to photograph using digital cameras. Anyone old enough to have been doing 35mm film photography can tell you that Tri-X was a popular black and white negative film made by Kodak. I shot with black and white because B&W film costs less, and I could develop and make prints myself without the higher cost and technical challenges of processing and printing in color. So I never photographed with color as motivation.

It was always composition, texture, and lighting that mattered. Decades of using black and white film trained me to look for images in monochrome. I loved the aesthetic, and I was inspired by Eugene Smith, Bruce Davidson, Roy DeCarava, and Danny Lyon. When using film, I did not push the shutter with color in mind.
